Dr. A . P . J ABDUL KALAM

1931 - 2015

Bharat Ratna Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied physics and aerospace engineering. For the next forty years, Kalam focused on science and administration, notably at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He played a vital role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning the nickname "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. Additionally, he held important roles during India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, marking a crucial milestone in the country's nuclear capabilities.

Kalam's writings

  • In 1988, A P J Abdul Kalam and Roddam Narasimha wrote a book named "Advancements in Fluid Mechanics and Space Technology," and it was published by the Indian Academy of Sciences.
  • In 1999, a book called "Wings of Fire: An Autobiography" was written by A P J Abdul Kalam and Arun Tiwari. It was published by Universities Press.
  • In 2002, A P J Abdul Kalam wrote a book called "Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India," and it was published by Viking.
  • In 2004, Punya Publishing Pvt Ltd. released a book called "The Luminous Sparks" written by A P J Abdul Kalam.
  • In 2005, Penguin Books published a book called "Mission India," written by A P J Abdul Kalam and featuring paintings by Manav Gupta.
  • In 2011, Ocean Books released a book titled "You Are Born To Blossom: Take My Journey Beyond," co-authored by A P J Abdul Kalam and Arun Tiwari.
